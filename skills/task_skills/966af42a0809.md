## 5. Common Pitfalls
*   **Data Preprocessing:** Ensure data is stationary (use differencing if needed). Consider transformations (log, box-cox) for variance stabilization.
*   **ARIMA Order Selection:** The `order` parameter (p, d, q) is crucial. Use techniques like AIC, BIC, or ACF/PACF plots to determine appropriate values.  Incorrect order selection will lead to poor forecasts.
*   **Train/Test Split:** Maintain temporal order. Do not shuffle the data.
*   **ABM Integration:**  Ensure the ABM outputs forecasts in a compatible format (e.g., a list or array).
*   **Evaluation Metric:** RMSE is common, but consider other metrics like MAE or MAPE depending on the specific application.
*   **Data Leakage:** Ensure no future information is used to train the ARIMA model.
