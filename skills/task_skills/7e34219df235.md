## Common Pitfalls
*   **Incorrect State Representation:**  A poorly defined state will prevent effective learning. Ensure the state captures relevant environmental factors.
*   **Missing Q-Table Initialization:** Ensure Q-values are initialized (e.g., to 0) when a state is first encountered.
*   **Exploitation Before Exploration:** Ensure sufficient exploration happens before relying heavily on exploitation. Decay the exploration rate over time.
*   **Reward Scaling:** Ensure rewards are appropriately scaled to provide meaningful feedback to the agent. Large rewards can lead to instability; small rewards can hinder learning.
*   **Forgetting to Update:** Failing to call `update_q_table` after each action will prevent learning.
*   **Incorrect Action Selection:**  Double-check that the `choose_action` method correctly implements exploration and exploitation.
*   **Off-by-one errors:** When calculating the temporal difference, ensure you're using the *next* state's *best* action Q-value.
