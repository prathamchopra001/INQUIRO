## Common Pitfalls
*   **Incorrect Data Collection:** Ensure you are collecting the correct variables (price, size) at each step.
*   **Poorly Defined Consumer Choice:** The consumer choice logic significantly impacts results. Experiment with different choice models (e.g., based on price, firm size, or a combination).
*   **Lack of Parameterization:** Make the model configurable (number of agents, grid size, simulation length).
*   **Ignoring Edge Cases:** Handle cases where firms have zero demand or extremely high prices.
*   **Not Visualizing Results:** Use histograms and other plots to understand the distribution of pricing strategies.
*   **Inefficient Matching:** Naive matching algorithms will slow down the simulation.
