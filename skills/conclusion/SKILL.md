# SKILL.md

## Task Description  
This role synthesizes research findings into a coherent conclusion, highlighting key insights, implications, and future directions. It ensures the conclusion aligns with the research objective and provides actionable takeaways for the audience.

## Output Format  
A structured written conclusion with three core sections:  
1. **Summary of Key Findings** (2–3 concise statements)  
2. **Implications & Significance** (Contextualize impact, e.g., "This work bridges X and Y")  
3. **Future Directions** (2–3 specific recommendations, e.g., "Prioritize Z to address gaps in...")  

## Decision Framework  
1. **Prioritize Depth Over Breadth**: Focus on 2–3 most impactful findings, not all details.  
2. **Confidence Scoring**: Assign confidence levels (High/Medium/Low) to claims based on data availability.  
   - High: Directly supported by empirical evidence.  
   - Medium: Inferred from patterns, not standalone data.  
   - Low: Hypothetical or speculative.  
3. **Edge Case Handling**:  
   - If findings are conflicting, acknowledge discrepancies and suggest further research.  
   - If data is limited, avoid overgeneralizing and state constraints explicitly.  

## Common Patterns  
### Example 1  
**Input**: "Research on AI ethics frameworks for healthcare."  
**Output**:  
"Key findings include: (1) AI ethics frameworks improve transparency in diagnostic tools (High confidence). (2) Patient privacy remains a critical gap (Medium confidence). Implications: These frameworks can enhance trust in AI-driven healthcare. Future directions: Develop region-specific guidelines and integrate real-time audits."  

### Example 2  
**Input**: "Study on renewable energy adoption in rural communities."  
**Output**:  
"Key findings: (1) Solar adoption rates correlate with microgrid access (High). (2) Policy barriers outweigh financial costs (Medium). Implications: Tailored policies can accelerate adoption. Future directions: Pilot community-led financing models and expand grid connectivity."  

### Example 3  
**Input**: "Analysis of remote work productivity across industries."  
**Output**:  
"Key findings: (1) Tech sectors report 15% productivity gains (High). (2) Creative fields face collaboration challenges (Medium). Implications: Remote work benefits vary by role. Future directions: Standardize hybrid work metrics and invest in virtual collaboration tools."  

## Anti-Patterns  
- **Vague Statements**: "This research is important" → Replace with specific impacts.  
- **Ignoring Objectives**: Including tangential topics not tied to the research goal.  
- **Overpromising**: Claiming universal applicability without caveats (e.g., "Solutions work everywhere").  
- **Missing Structure**: Outputs that lack clear sectioning or flow.  

## Quality Checklist  
1. **Alignment**: Does the conclusion directly address the research objective?  
2. **Clarity**: Are findings, implications, and future directions clearly separated?  
3. **Specificity**: Are recommendations actionable and data-driven?  
4. **Balance**: Does it acknowledge limitations without undermining key points?  
5. **Conciseness**: Is each section within 2–3 sentences?
